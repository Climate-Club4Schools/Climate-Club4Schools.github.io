1. What is Climate?

  • OK, so start at the beginning - what is Climate? it's connected to weather, but not the same. These slides help explain some basics of why the world has weather, and how climate refers to the long-term average of weather. We intuitively know that some places are hot or cold, dry pr wet - discuss this with the class and see if they can name some places for each category.

  • Bonus points if they can talk about the less obvious feeatures: e.g. hot places are generally near the equator - but what are the exceptions? Mt. Everest is pretty cold, despite being in the tropics. Or when talking about places where it rains a lot - don't forget over the ocean! or deserts can be cold - the Antarctic gets very little precipitation

  • The you can talk about seasons - climate is more than just the average for the year. Some places are characterised by hot and cold seasons, or maybe wet and dry seasons. There are som examples to go over - can the class guess where each of these places is by the characteristics of the seasons - feel free to add or replace with examples relevant to you

  • At the end of the lesson introduce the activity for the next few weeks - you're going to make some Top Trumps cards. See the section below for more details on this activity...

    • 2. Why is Climate Changing?

  • Once we know what climate is we can discuss why it changes. We know it cn change naturally - over millions of years there are numerous natural causes from moving continents to changing orbits, volcanoes and meteor strikes. But _now_ it is changing much faster and it can only be human-caused. We know the basic physics too well to leave any uncertainty here

  • These slides introduce the idea of greenhouse gases and where they come from - primarily CO2. It comes from all of our activity - mainly now from burning fuels, but previously lots from deforestation too. There is more CO2 in the atmosphere now than for at least a million years. Some catchy animations show how our emissions have increased and so has global temperature

  • Kids love fun facts and figures - your car can drink a can-of-coke's worth of petrol every 3 minutes. Try doing that yourself! Then imagine all the cars on all the roads. It quickly adds up to a crushing amount of carbon being burned.
