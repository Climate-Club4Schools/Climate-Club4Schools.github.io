Course 1 overview

This is intended to be the primary Climate Club course. If you end up running more than one per year and have children who have already done this, then course 2 provides new content and ideas. Of course you're free to mix and match and use bits of both.

Each course is intended to run for one term - so approximately 10-12 weeks. I recommend one lesson per week for around 45 minutes. 1 hour is maximum - I find this is a little too long - both for the available content and the kids' attention span. But again, tailor to your needs and availability. Some schools may prefer to run this before school, or as an after-school club.

The structure of Course 1 is:

  • What is climate?
    • 2 weeks discussing concepts of weather and climate and how they differ and the start of a Top Trumps activity
  • Why does climate change?
    • 2 weeks discussing why climate may change - both naturally but also due to human influence. Talk about fossil fuels, greenhouse gases and finish the Top Trumps

I find it works well to alternate weeks between delivering content via some slides - make these sessions informal and lots of discussion, but also cover a fair amount of content; and then weeks of activity - making the Top Trumps or display items or planning for an assembly.

  • What impacts is it having?
    • 2 weeks discussing some of the impacts of climate change - especially on ecosystems. For this age group I recommend avoiding doom and gloom of famine and flood which cause large numbers of human casualties
  • What are the solutions?
    • This includes discussion over mis/dis-information and the importance of knowing facts. Then coverage of solutions - both large scale and local scale things we can all do

Course 2 overview

Course 2 is a follow-on from course 1, but can stand-alone so that it is not vital to have attended course 1. The intention though is to use course 1 by default and use course 2 as and when you have a cohort repeating. It starts with a quick summary from course 1 but then goes into deeper detail of climate changes, our "climate history" and then our "climate future" with a strong focus on learning more details of available solutions.
